DESIGN STATEMENT

There are many ways to connect with people, in-person or through a device. There are also many different kinds of companionship. In response to the COVID-19 home working protocol, I created a sculpture to lessen the loneliness felt in the stillness of no activities. The animal-like behavior makes the sculpture feel almost like a living companion, and this companion moves unobtrusively when no human voices are present. When human voices are present, the sculpture displays a more exciting behavior in response. This way, the presence of others is abstracted through sound and movement.

AUDIENCE

For people who live alone or prefers to be alone, but want a subtle companion that reacts to them, while not requiring them to give responses.

IDEATION & DESIGN

I decided to use the accordion paper fan design as a moving unit because the opening of the structure allows a simple servo rotation to make a big visual effect.

The garland form was chosen as the final design because circle symbolizes interconnection and fulfillment.

PROCESS

It wasn't a smooth process realizing my design, because the distance of the servos, where the connection point is, or number of folds on the paper would all affect how the sculpture behaves. Additionally, a lot of testing was done on the parameters in the code to make the movement have the ideal speed and consistency.

The sound responsive part was coded on Processing, and then connected to Arduino IDE to control the servos. Servos were connected to the accordion paper fan with wires, the wires are hidden in the fold and are unnoticeable when viewed from a distance.
